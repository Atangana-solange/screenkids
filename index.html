👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Dear parents, welcome to ScreenKids!

In today's digital world, screens are everywhere — smartphones, tablets, computers, televisions and video game consoles surround our children from a very young age. While technology offers real educational opportunities, excessive screen time can affect children's health, sleep, concentration and social development.

ScreenKids helps you track, understand and analyse how much time your children spend in front of screens each day. By collecting real data, you can identify patterns and make informed decisions for your family.

The WHO 🏥 recommends no more than 60 minutes per day for children aged 3–5. This app classifies risk levels automatically using those guidelines.

Fill in all six fields for each observation then click Add record. Add as many records as you want — for different children, different days or different devices. The more data, the richer the analysis!

🏥 WHO Recommendations

Under 2 years: Avoid screens (except video calls).
2–4 years: Max 1 hour/day of quality content.
5–17 years: Limit recreational use — prioritise sleep, physical activity and social interaction.

💡 Did you know?

Children with more than 2 hours/day of screen time show higher rates of sleep disturbances, attention difficulties and lower academic performance. Tracking is the first step toward a healthier balance!
