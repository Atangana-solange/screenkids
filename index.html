👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Dear parents, welcome to ScreenKids!

In today's digital world, screens are everywhere — smartphones 📱, tablets 📟, computers 🖥️, televisions 📺 and video game consoles 🎮 surround our children from a very young age. While technology offers real educational opportunities, excessive or unregulated screen time can have significant effects on children's health, sleep, concentration and social development.

ScreenKids is designed to help you track, understand and analyse how much time your children spend in front of screens each day 📊. By collecting real data, you can identify patterns, compare different devices and activities, and make informed decisions for your family 🏡.

The World Health Organization (WHO) 🏥 recommends no more than 60 minutes per day for children aged 3–5, and balanced, quality screen use for older children. This app classifies risk levels automatically using those guidelines ✅.

📖 How to use

Fill in all six fields for each child observation, then click Add record. You can add as many records as you want — for different children, different days, or different devices. The more data, the richer the analysis! 🎉

🏥 WHO Screen Time Recommendations

🔹 Under 2 years: Avoid screens (except video calls).
🔹 2–4 years: Max 1 hour/day of quality content.
🔹 5–17 years: Limit recreational use; prioritise sleep 😴, physical activity 🏃 and social interaction 👫.

💡 Did you know?

Children with more than 2 hours/day of screen time show higher rates of sleep disturbances 😴, attention difficulties 🧠 and lower academic performance 📉. Tracking is the first step toward a healthier balance! ❤️
